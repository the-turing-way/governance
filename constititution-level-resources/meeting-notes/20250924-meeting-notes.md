## Applying to represent The Turing Way, or include it on a grant/fellowship
* Affiliations with the Turing Way for Fellowship applications
  * The Turing Way accepts applications to submit/apply for things in the future as a form of partnership. There are no set rules for the project category to apply for.
  * Develop a "pre code of conduct" to safeguard against bad actors in the future.
  * For example, a fellowship where the fellow does work which is not aligned with the wider community
* Transparency is important. The Community should know that you are making use of the Turing Way in your applications.
* How can these safeguards be applied to fellowship schemes that are not as familiar with TTW as SSI?
* Alternative approach: take a hand's off approach. It is up to the application to make sure their proposal of collaborating with the community is realistic
  * Our current approach is more like this, relying on people to reach out to the community, share ideas and discuss
  * A downside to this is it takes some confidence to put your idea out before the community and take the lead in engaging us
* Documenting how an applicant can get a TTW endorsement is advancing best practices to empower community members to contribute to an open source project
* BUT making it mandatory to need an endorsement, is counterintuitive for the purpose of the project
* Then naturally, in which cases do we require endorsement and when do we not?
  * Sometimes we feel like the community or steering committee SHOULD have a say on endorsing
  * For example, the response to UKRI on data policy
  * The key difference is '*representing*' the Turing Way or contributing to the Turing Way

## Community Forums
* There was confusion about who is responsible for organising community forums
* Previously it was the role of a dedicated community manager
* Many of those responsibilities now sit with the CMWG
* Argument that as a strategically important event, the steering committee should take responsibility
* Some history
  * A special arrangement was made for the June 2025 community forum
  * CMWG set the date for the September 2025 community forum, but did not assume responsibility for the event
* CMWG
  * Feeling overwhelmed by project maintenance (GitHub)
  * Fluctuation in capacity
  * Great effort from members, but this is volunteer effort
  * Clashes with tasks that cannot be delegated (for example, running events, sending newsletters)
  * Difficult to call on people/assign tasks when people are volunteering
  * Onboarding more members
  * Hopeful that better documentation will help
  * Risk of CMWG burn out!
* Discussion on options going forward 
* We [CMWG] have tried to keep all existing activities going, with only volunteer effort. The project should be open to changing things to reduce stress on CMWG and make things sustainable for volunteers. For example,
  * Less regular newsletters/updates
  * Simplify social media (or suspend)
* CMWG should assess what are the most important things, or essential activities to maintain, what can scale with capacity, what are nice-to-haves
* **Proposal:** make the steering committee responsible, accountability is held by the chair
  * No objections  üéâ
* Steering committee will take ownership of community forums
  * An opportunity for the governance level to do something to celebrate the contributions of all community members
  * The purpose of the community forum is to engage contributions across all of the community
  * That feels well within the remit of the governance level
